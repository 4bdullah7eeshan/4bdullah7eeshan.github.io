My understanding of the things we can blog about.

Blogging is highly encouraged and advised especially if we are in tech. If we decide to start a blog, the question that comes naturally is What should we blog about?.

In search of the answers for this specific question, I did a bit of research.

This post lays out a summary of the research I did and my understanding of some of the various things that we can blog about.

There are more things we can blog about than the ones listed here. But I picked the ones that seemed more relevant and serve as a general use case.

In total, there are five things mentioned here.

They are as follows:

  1. The Feynman Technique
  2. Sharing our leaning/coding journey
  3. Sharing great resources
  4. Creating summaries
  5. Most common How-tos

For each of these above items, I layout the following:

  1. A brief description of it
  2. Some Pros
  3. Some Cons
    1. Some ways to overcome them

At the end, I share a few resources.

The Feynman Technique

This is essentially, attempting to teach whatever we have learned through our post by writing about it.

Pros

  • Helps us understand, organize, and synthesis our knowledge for us
  • Reveals gaps in our understanding
  • Provides our own unique perspective of the topic for the learners

Cons

Time Consuming

This process takes a lot of time. We could just think in our minds and attempt to teach it by speaking out loud or just thinking without having to write. If our thinking skills are sharp, that will be enough. Also, when we learn something new, instead of spending time on writing a blog post about it, we could move on and learn newer things for ourselves.

Overcoming Tips
  • Instead of writing a blog post of everything that we learn, we can choose to write a post on topics that are considered hard or not so easy to grasp. This way, we end up writing fewer posts but filled with quality.

Risk of being wrong and not qualified

There are high chances that the things, we attempt to teach will have flaws in it. We might be wrong on some matters or our understanding might not be accurate. We are learners at the end of the day and not qualified in any form, yet!

Overcoming Tips
  • Don’t come from a place of being an expert on the topic.
  • Let the readers know that we have no authority or official expertise.
  • Let the readers know that we are just sharing our own understanding of the topic.
  • Let them know that we can be wrong.

Sharing our learning/coding journey

Here, we essentially maintain a log of our learning/coding journey. The ups and downs, the gotcha moments, and so on.

Pros

  • Good self-reflection practice
  • Helps learners who are a few steps behind us. I myself have personally learned a lot from other people’s experiences and journeys.

Cons

Susceptible to vulnerability.

Well not exactly a cons depending on how we look it. Some of us might think that being vulnerable and sharing our journeys is a sign of courage and maturity. Our vulnerable moments on the wrong hands might be something to think about. People who have a good heart and are wise will understand all this and not ridicule us.

Overcoming Tips

I am not going to step hard on this as it is a matter of personal choice.

Currently, the way I look at this is as follows: We must be able to showcase our learning weaknesses and our vulnerable side to the world. Everyone sucks when they begin. All the great people sucked so damn badly. Being not good enough is not something to be ashamed of. What do we do next?! How do we improve?! That is what matters.

Sharing great resources

Along our learning journeys, we discover many new resources. Sometimes underrated ones that are hidden gems. We think, “Gosh! Why do people not know about this?!”. We can curate a list of such resources and share it in our blog post.

Pros

  • A record for ourselves, if we need it. However, I personally, was advised by my mentor to not rely on it and start from fresh and research again to discover even newer and amazing resources.
  • Helping the resource creators’s work reach out to more people. They will appreciate it!

Cons

Subjective Nature

  • The resources, we found amazing might not be amazing to our readers. Everyone has their own taste.
Overcoming Tips

Again we don’t enforce this list to anyone, we just let our readers know that “Hey! I found these resources helpful when I was learning. Maybe you can try them out for yourself.”

Creating summaries

This is essentially creating short summaries of long form content (both offline as well as online) such as the following:

  • Videos
  • Books
  • Conferences
  • Talks
  • Classes

Pros

  • Capturing the essence of an source
  • Ensuring strong grasp over what was said.

Cons

Note-taking trap

We might fall into the trap of writing lengthy notes.

Overcoming Tips
  • Keep it short and simple
  • Provide our perspectives on the source

Most common How-tos

As a coder, there are many things that we do regularly almost everyday as part of our workflow. They barely change. They are fixed routine tasks. Like, setting up our JavaScript projects, setting up a cloud database, etc. It would be better to have them listed and simplified at a place instead of going back to the resources and figuring out every time.

Pros

  • Serves as a quick cheat sheet

Cons

Subjective Nature

These will be our workflows and might not be applicable to our readers.

Overcoming Tips
  • Explain a brief reasoning behind the steps. If our readers align with our philosophy and background, they can adopt it.

My Summary

Based on my current scenario, I have decided to blog about the following three things:

  1. Sharing my learning/coding journey
  2. Most common How-tos
  3. Sharing great resources
